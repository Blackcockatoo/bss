# Campaign Copy Style Guide

## Purpose
Use this guide for all updates to landing, schools, and elevator pitch copy so messaging stays bold, clear, and appropriate for school leaders, funders, and compliance audiences.

## Tone
- **Bold but grounded:** confident statements supported by outcomes, implementation detail, or architecture.
- **Neutral-professional:** avoid inflammatory language, personal attacks, or slang aimed at competitors.
- **Audience-aware:** school-facing copy should emphasize classroom practicality, student safety, and governance readiness.
- **Evidence-oriented:** when using strong claims, pair them with qualifiers (e.g., "pilot scope", "benchmark", "aligned to").

## Banned / Avoid Phrases
Avoid wording that can sound derogatory, alienating, or combative:
- "keeps its mouth shut"
- "farming your children"
- "desperate for this"
- "stupider for not already being in"
- "data extraction operation" (without contextual qualifier)
- "we're the answer" / "we're the blueprint" style absolute claims
- Any phrase that shames schools, families, or vendors

Prefer neutral alternatives:
- "privacy-first"
- "designed to protect student privacy"
- "aligned to emerging compliance guidance"
- "reduces privacy risk and operational overhead"

## Approved Terminology (Cross-Page)
Use these terms consistently across **landing**, **schools**, and **elevator** pages:

### Privacy architecture
- **Zero-Collection Educational Architecture (ZCEA)**
- **privacy-first architecture**
- **on-device pilot**
- **no student accounts**
- **no student data collection**
- **no student data transmission**
- **privacy-by-design**

### Classroom benefits
- **classroom-ready**
- **curriculum-aligned**
- **ready to implement**
- **minimal IT lift**
- **teacher-supported delivery**
- **calm interaction design**

### Outcomes and governance
- **wellbeing and STEM outcomes**
- **student agency and reflection**
- **reduced compliance overhead**
- **school privacy governance readiness**
- **aligned to COPC 2025 direction**

## Style Rules
- Prefer **student** over **kid** in school/compliance contexts.
- Prefer **families** or **parents and carers** over casual audience labels when possible.
- Use "zero" claims only with clear scope (e.g., "in pilot scope").
- Keep contrast framing factual: avoid "unlike every other" unless empirically supported.
- Maintain short, high-impact headings; keep supporting paragraphs concrete and specific.

## Pre-Publish Copy Check
Before merging copy updates:
1. Scan for banned phrasing and remove emotionally loaded wording.
2. Confirm privacy terms match approved terminology.
3. Confirm classroom benefit language appears on landing + schools + pitch.
4. Confirm outcomes language includes both learning impact and governance/compliance value.
